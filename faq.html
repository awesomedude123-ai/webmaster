FAQ

How do I schedule a virtual check-up?

At WorldMed Communications, scheduling a check-up can be done right from your couch. Simply follow three simple steps:

1. Fill out the check-up form located on the Virtual Check-Ups Page under Services
2. Read the instructions sent to you via email and phone to schedule a check-up time slot
3. Sit back and relax! Scheduling is free, and there are no deductibles or co-pays!

Is my medical information secure?

Any medical information you give us is stored safely with WorldMed Communications. Along with leveraging a cloud secured data storage network to safeguard your medical records, we’ve partnered with reliable data storage providers with security features to prevent data theft.

Is the quality of telemedicine worse than that of in-person healthcare?

Without a doubt, WorldMed Communications provides consistent and quality healthcare, medical attention, treatment options, and news for you to stay in the know about your health status. Our 24/7 specialists and health professional network ensure a safe, cost-effective, and easy way to provide quality, if not better, healthcare services to you.

Who will be treating me?

WorldMed Communications has partnered with experienced and knowledgeable health professionals from around the globe to give you the best possible healthcare experience. As a result, we are able to offer services in many different health specialties. Our doctors and 24/7 specialists are top in the nation in what they do, and they’re determined to help you.

Will insurance pay for my video visits?

Yes! Many insurance companies are able to pay for telemedicine visits at WorldMed Communications, and some states have laws dictating this. Please be up to date with your state’s regulations. However, WorldMed Communications offers free virtual check-ups for your first consultation!

Are my video visits private?

Yes! We operate under an encrypted platform as we’ve partnered with Vonage to bring you quality video conferencing experiences. All visits are not recorded and all information discussed is kept confidential. We do not operate under consumer applications such as Facebook, Zoom, or Skype.

Are telemedicine services difficult to use?

WorldMed Communication’s software, including our virtual check-up sign-up process, as well as our treatment plan registration, is all easy and quick to do. Our software, especially our mobile application, is up to date and user-friendly.
